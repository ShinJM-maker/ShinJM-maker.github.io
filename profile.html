Biography

Multimodal AI Researcher for Structured Evidence and Reliable Reasoning

I research how AI systems recover, organize, and audit evidence from heterogeneous unstructured observations.

My current work focuses on structure-aware evidence systems for documents and long-context reasoning.

I also founded and lead KUDoc, a document AI research team within Korea University’s NLP & AI Lab, where I help drive research on document understanding, multimodal RAG, evaluation, and applied AI systems.

Looking ahead, I aim to extend this foundation toward structured memory, world models, and planning for future agents.

This trajectory moves from linguistic structure to document structure, and toward dynamic world structure.

Research Map

Quick overview of how my research evolves from linguistic structure and multimodal evidence systems toward structured memory and future agents.

Stage 1 (2021-2022)

Core Linguistic Structure and Symbolic Reasoning

Dependency parsing, neural-symbolic modeling, table QA, and lexical-semantic structure.

Stage 2 (2023-2024)

Applied LLMs and Domain Evidence Systems

Zero-shot analysis, domain-specific RAG, chatbot systems, and industrial evidence pipelines.

Stage 3 (2025-2026)

Multimodal Evidence Systems

M3DocDep (CVPR 2026), HiKEY (ACL 2026), MultiDocFusion (EMNLP 2025), and related work on multimodal structure recovery, evidence assembly, and long-context reasoning.

Stage 4 (2027+)

Toward Structured Memory, World Models, and Agents

Actionable ontologies, executable planning representations, and video-structured memory for long-horizon multimodal agents.
